# CryptoPayMap public product roadmap

## Purpose

This user-facing roadmap describes product capabilities through four sections:

```text
Now
Next
Later
Exploring
```

It is not the repository implementation plan. It does not publish private deadlines, pull-request counts, internal queue metrics, business targets, or private launch thresholds.

Published record counts belong in Stats. Record changes belong in Updates. Released product changes belong in Changelog.

---

## Status meanings

- **In progress:** actively being developed or validated.
- **Planned:** accepted direction, not necessarily active yet.
- **Completed:** released and linked to a Changelog entry.
- **Under consideration:** exploration, not a commitment.
- **Revised:** moved, merged, replaced, or otherwise changed with a public explanation.

---

# Now

## Verified data foundation

**Status:** In progress  
**Last updated:** 2026-06-26

### Outcome

Create a public data foundation that distinguishes reviewed acceptance from unverified source material and explains how a customer can attempt payment.

### Includes

- canonical merchants, places, and online services;
- explicit asset and network combinations;
- direct-wallet and processor-checkout routes;
- payment methods and How to pay instructions;
- evidence, confirmation dates, and history;
- confirmed, stale, and ended states;
- provenance and licensing;
- private candidate separation;
- validated public JSON and GeoJSON;
- protected review and publication workflow.

### Depends on

- public data and verification contracts;
- canonical registries and database;
- review and publication validation.

# Next

## Core discovery experience

**Status:** Planned  
**Last updated:** 2026-06-26

### Outcome

Make verified physical places and online services easy to discover and understand on mobile and desktop.

### Includes

- Home discovery entry;
- coordinated Places map and list;
- Search this area;
- asset, network, category, route, and status filters;
- mobile Map / List mode;
- selected-place bottom sheet;
- shareable and restorable URL state;
- browser-back restoration;
- place and service details;
- Stats, Updates, Roadmap, and Changelog;
- accessibility and performance baselines;
- installable web-app manifest.

### Depends on

- verified data foundation;
- responsive application shell;
- public artifact delivery;
- mapping and accessibility implementation.

# Later

## Community contribution and business claims

**Status:** Planned  
**Last updated:** 2026-06-26

### Outcome

Allow customers and businesses to improve records without permitting automatic or unreviewed public edits.

### Includes

- suggestions;
- payment reports;
- problem and closure reports;
- business and service claims;
- ownership verification;
- photo submissions;
- private submission-status access;
- requests for information;
- time-bounded holds;
- field-level partial approval;
- media quarantine, rights review, privacy review, and public derivatives;
- canonical transaction and publication validation.

### Depends on

- verified data foundation;
- core discovery experience;
- submission security;
- media processing;
- administration and audit workflow.

---

## Data access and business tools

**Status:** Planned  
**Last updated:** 2026-06-26

### Outcome

Make verified payment-acceptance data easier to reuse and make it easier for businesses to maintain accurate records.

### Includes

- documented public data files;
- stable schemas and versions;
- public change feeds;
- broader data-access options;
- business record maintenance;
- multi-location management;
- processor and wallet integration paths;
- clearly disclosed partnership surfaces that remain independent from verification.

### Depends on

- stable public schema;
- contribution and ownership workflows;
- authorization and audit controls;
- published data and license policy.

# Exploring

Exploring items are under consideration and are not implementation commitments.

## Indirect spending guides

**Status:** Under consideration

Separately labeled guides may cover card-funded spending, vouchers, and bill-payment intermediaries. These routes would remain outside the main Places and Online Services directories and would not be described as direct merchant cryptocurrency acceptance.

## Accounts and saved places

**Status:** Under consideration

Optional accounts, favorites, recently viewed records, and saved searches, subject to privacy and data-minimization review.

## Notifications

**Status:** Under consideration

Opt-in notices for changes to followed records or payment routes, with clear consent and bounded retention.

## Multilingual support

**Status:** Under consideration

Reviewed translations for discovery, payment instructions, methodology, and contribution flows without changing canonical identities or weakening verification.

## Regional verification collaboration

**Status:** Under consideration

Publicly disclosed regional collaboration that improves local evidence and reconfirmation while preserving the same verification and conflict rules.

## Advanced change detection

**Status:** Under consideration

Tools that identify changes in approved public sources. Detected changes would create private review candidates, never automatic public updates.

## Limited offline support

**Status:** Under consideration

An offline application shell and clearly dated recently viewed records. Old payment data would not be silently presented as current.

## Roadmap change policy

A roadmap item may be:

- completed and linked to a Changelog release;
- revised with a public explanation;
- merged into another milestone;
- moved to Exploring;
- marked No longer planned.

Items are not removed silently after appearing as planned work.

Public explanations use product, data-quality, security, privacy, accessibility, maintenance, or dependency reasons. They do not expose private internal circumstances.
